History of Breast Cancer Awareness

1970s

First Wave of Awareness

The breast cancer movement began when First Lady Betty Ford openly discussed her diagnosis in 1974, bringing national attention to the cause and encouraging women to speak about their experiences.

1982

Pink Ribbon Movement

Susan G. Komen for the Cure and other organizations popularized pink ribbons and large-scale fundraising events, turning awareness into action.

1985

Awareness Month Established

October became recognized as Breast Cancer Awareness Month, leading to annual campaigns, education drives, and a surge in research funding.

1990s

Genetic Discoveries

Discovery of the BRCA1 and BRCA2 genes transformed understanding of hereditary breast cancer and informed screening strategies for high-risk individuals.

2000s

Global Movement & Better Outcomes

Improved screening, targeted therapies, and public awareness led to higher survival rates worldwide and a growing global support network.

Today

Modern Research & Care

Today, precision medicine, immunotherapy, and improved diagnostics continue to advance care. Awareness campaigns focus on equity, access to care, and survivorship.
